1. **Project Objective**
    The goal of this task was to develop a MATLAB script capable of transforming a mathematical radiation pattern into a three-dimensional visual model. This is a critical step in RF engineering to evaluate the spatial characteristics and directivity of different antenna types.
    
2. **Findings**
- The Omni-Directional Pattern: For this experiment, the Field Pattern = 1.
- Shape: The resulting plot is a perfect sphere because the signal strength is identical in every direction.
- Range: This simulated a full coverage area by setting both Theta and Phi from 0 to 360 degrees.
- Application: This represents an ideal omni-directional source, designed to provide equal coverage to all surrounding users.

3. **Conclusion**
    In wireless deployment, 3D visualization is helpful for verifying antenna directivity. While a sphere provides 360 degree coverage, most practical antennas trade that uniformity for Gain, focusing energy in specific directions to increase range.
